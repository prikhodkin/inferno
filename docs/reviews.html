    Burning Man 2019: Sneak Peek At This Years Outrageous Art Installations

    Created by Nevada based mosaic artist Peter Hazel, Niloticus is a 40-foot long hand made mosaic tiled crocodile that invites visitors to climb on top of him. The features include hand-blown glass teeth and osteoderms that will light up in the night.

    According to the artist, "Niloticus, or Tim as I call him, is the fourth water creature I have created for Burning Man. When I first attended Burning Man in 2013, I was taken by the expanse of the playa. The alkaline desert evoked a beach to me; I saw giant sea creatures rising from it. As a mosaic artist and diver, the fluidity of sea creatures appealed to me. So, I started creating them. Metamorphoses, Ovid’s epic poem, is literally Books of Transformations. I see art, at its essence, as transformation. I take clay and glass and through heat transform them into tiles which in turn are transformed into giant creatures. Niloticus, an ancient mythical creature, invites people to consider their scaly exterior and explore their personal transformations. How hard are their scales? When did they develop? Do they want/need to harden them more or soften them? What transformation are they experiencing? What further transformation do they seek?"
